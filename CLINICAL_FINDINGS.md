# Clinical Findings & Medical Interpretation

**Cardio-Analytics Project — Medical Informatics Portfolio**

This document interprets the data findings from a clinical perspective,
explaining what the numbers mean for patient care, lab performance,
and cardiology practice.

---

## Procedure Volume Trends

**Finding:** The laboratory performed a consistently high
volume of procedures across 2022–2025, with seasonal dips in August
and December.

**Clinical meaning:** August dips reflect reduced elective scheduling
during the summer holiday period. December dips reflect the same
pattern around Christmas. This is expected and normal for any European
hospital. The fact that emergency procedures (primary PCI for heart
attacks) continued uninterrupted during these months confirms the lab
maintained 24/7 emergency capacity — a core requirement for one of the biggest
hospitals in the country.

---

## Angioplasty Rate (~37% of angiograms)

**Finding:** Approximately 37 out of every 100 patients who had a
diagnostic coronary angiogram also underwent an immediate intervention
(angioplasty/PCI) in the same session.

**Clinical meaning:** This is called the "ad hoc PCI rate". European
guidelines consider 30–40% a healthy range. Too low suggests the lab
is not capturing patients who need intervention. Too high suggests
patients may be intervened upon without sufficient diagnostic workup.
A 37% rate indicates appropriate patient selection and a well-functioning
diagnostic pathway.

---

## Primary PCI Rate (~11% of interventions)

**Finding:** 11% of all interventional procedures were emergency primary
PCI — performed on patients arriving with an acute ST-elevation
myocardial infarction (STEMI, i.e. a full heart attack).

**Clinical meaning:** Primary PCI is the gold-standard treatment for
STEMI and must be performed within 90 minutes of patient arrival
(door-to-balloon time). The volume seen here is consistent with the
expected STEMI incidence for a regional Greek hospital serving a
population of several hundred thousand people. Maintaining this
capacity requires around-the-clock staffing of the cath lab,
interventional cardiologists, and nursing staff.

---

## STEMI Mortality Rate (~2%)

**Finding:** Of patients undergoing primary PCI for STEMI, approximately
2% did not survive.

**Clinical meaning:** Published European data (EuroCTO Club, EUROMAX
trial) reports in-hospital mortality after primary PCI of 2–5%.
A 2% rate places this lab at the better end of the European benchmark.
This reflects fast door-to-balloon times, experienced operators,
and good post-procedural care. It is also a direct measure of how
many lives the lab saves — primary PCI reduces STEMI mortality from
~30% (untreated) to ~2–5%.

---

## CTO Procedures

**Finding:** A subset of angioplasty cases involved Chronic Total
Occlusions — arteries that have been completely blocked for more
than 3 months.

**Clinical meaning:** CTO-PCI is the most technically demanding
procedure in interventional cardiology. It requires specialised
operators, longer procedure times, higher radiation doses, and more
contrast dye. The presence of CTO cases in this dataset indicates
the lab has advanced expertise. Many hospitals refer CTO patients
elsewhere. Performing them in-house reflects a high-volume,
high-skill centre.

---

## Advanced Imaging Adoption (IVUS, OCT, FFR)

**Finding:** Use of intravascular imaging (IVUS, OCT) and functional
assessment (FFR) increased year-on-year across the dataset.

**Clinical meaning:**
- **IVUS** (intravascular ultrasound) allows the operator to see inside
  the artery wall in real time, optimising stent sizing and placement.
- **OCT** (optical coherence tomography) provides even higher resolution
  images, particularly useful for complex stenting.
- **FFR** (fractional flow reserve) measures whether a narrowing is
  actually causing reduced blood flow before deciding to stent it —
  preventing unnecessary interventions.

The 2023 ESC guidelines on acute coronary syndromes now recommend
routine use of intracoronary imaging for complex PCI. The upward trend
in this dataset shows the lab is actively adopting guideline-directed
practice — a marker of quality improvement over time.

---

## Patient Risk Profile

**Finding:** Mean age 64, 68% male, 55% hypertensive, 35% diabetic,
40% smokers, 20% with a prior heart attack.

**Clinical meaning:** This is a classic high-risk cardiology population.
The male predominance reflects the earlier onset of coronary artery
disease in men (oestrogen is cardioprotective in premenopausal women).
These are not surprising findings for this patient population but they matter —
despite a traditionally heart-healthy diet, urbanisation and lifestyle
changes have driven metabolic disease rates upward over the past
two decades. The 20% prior MI rate indicates a significant proportion
of patients with established, recurrent coronary disease requiring
repeat intervention.

---

## Elective Procedure Success Rate (>92%)

**Finding:** Over 92% of elective interventions resulted in procedural
success with no major complications.

**Clinical meaning:** The accepted benchmark for elective PCI success
in high-volume European centres is 90–95%. This lab sits within that
range. The remaining 8% includes complications such as:
- No-reflow (the artery reopens but blood flow does not restore)
- Side branch occlusion
- Stent thrombosis
- Vascular access complications
- Contrast-induced nephropathy (kidney injury from dye)

These are recognised risks disclosed to patients during informed
consent. A complication rate below 10% for elective PCI is considered
acceptable and safe.

---

## Length of Stay (Mean ~3 days)

**Finding:** The average patient stayed approximately 3 days after
their procedure.

**Clinical meaning:** Uncomplicated elective PCI patients are typically
discharged the next morning (1 day). STEMI patients requiring
monitoring stay 3–5 days. Complex cases (CTO, TAVI, heart failure)
stay longer. A mean of 3 days across all procedures is consistent
with a mixed-acuity cath lab serving both elective and emergency
patients. Shorter stays reduce hospital-acquired infection risk and
cost. Longer stays, when clinically justified, reflect appropriate
post-procedure monitoring.

---

## TAVI and Structural Heart Disease

**Finding:** A small proportion of procedures were TAVI (transcatheter
aortic valve implantation) and PFO closures.

**Clinical meaning:** TAVI replaces a diseased aortic valve without
open-heart surgery, using a catheter threaded through the femoral
artery. It is now the first-line treatment for severe aortic stenosis
in patients over 75 or those with high surgical risk. PFO closure
seals a small hole between the heart's upper chambers, indicated
in young stroke patients. The presence of both procedures confirms
this is a full structural heart disease programme — not just a
coronary lab — placing it among the more comprehensive cardiac
centres in Greece.

---

## Summary

This dataset reflects a busy, well-performing cardiology
catheterisation laboratory with:

\-  High procedural volume and consistent emergency capacity

\-  Angioplasty and complication rates within European benchmarks

\-  Growing adoption of advanced imaging tools in line with current guidelines

\-  A complex patient population with multiple cardiovascular risk factors

\-  Advanced structural heart disease capabilities (TAVI, PFO, CTO)


The data tells the story of a modern cath lab delivering evidence-based
interventional cardiology to a high-risk population — and doing it well.
